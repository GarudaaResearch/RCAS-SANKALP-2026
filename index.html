Hackathon Themes

Theme 01
Future Fuels: Current Crisis
Address real-world challenges around energy, environment, climate, food security, water scarcity, and societal issues. Innovate solutions that matter — not for tomorrow, but for today's crisis.

Think: sustainable systems, smart resource management, disaster preparedness, pollution control, and public health.
☀️
Theme 02
Solar as Source
Harness the power of the sun. Design innovative products, systems, or services that use solar energy as a primary source to solve problems in agriculture, transportation, education, healthcare, or rural development.

Think: solar-powered IoT, off-grid solutions, clean mobility, smart agriculture, and sustainable infrastructure.

Rules & Guidelines
Participation Guidelines
📝
Idea Submission
  • Submit your idea abstract online before 17 Apr 2026, 5:00 PM
  • Abstract must be original and not previously submitted anywhere
  • Include problem statement, proposed solution, and expected impact
  • Maximum 500 words or 10 slides for the First Round submission
👥
Team Composition
  • Individual participants are welcome
  • Teams can have up to 4 members
  • Cross-department and inter-college teams are encouraged
  • Team registration fee: ₹400/- per team (regardless of team size)
⚙️
Final Hackathon Day
  • Selected teams must report by 9:00 AM on 24 April 2026
  • Bring your own laptop and required hardware/components
  • Teams will present a working prototype or demo
  • Judging based on innovation, feasibility, and impact
🏆
Judging Criteria
  • Innovation & Creativity (25%)
  • Technical Feasibility (25%)
  • Social / Environmental Impact (25%)
  • Presentation & Communication (25%)
🚫
Prohibited Practices
  • Plagiarism of any kind will lead to disqualification
  • Fully AI-generated solutions without human reasoning are not accepted
  • No use of others' proprietary data or intellectual property
  • Any form of misconduct will result in immediate elimination
📋
General Rules
  • All participants must carry valid college ID on the event day
  • Registration fees are non-refundable
  • Decision of the judges is final and binding
  • Organizers reserve rights to make changes with prior notice

⚠️ The SANKALP Principle — Human Intelligence First

SANKALP means a firm resolve. This hackathon is built on the resolve that human minds lead, and tools serve. While you may use AI as an assistant, the core ideas, problem framing, and solutions must reflect genuine human insight and creativity. We are building a future that is safe, meaningful, and human-led.

"Don't find the solution only using AI tools. Use human intelligence for human future safety."
